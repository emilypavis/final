I see you in my Dreams, a photobook by Emily Pavis

This semester at The New School I took a course at Eugene Lang College of Liberal Arts called Writing the Photobook. To read a description of this photography writing course, click here. The class, taught by Professor Robert Dunn, enabled me to both discover and explore my newfound passion for photography, specifically film. For my Web Fundamentals final, I wanted to create an online module spotlighting some of my favorite images that I took and curated for my photobook. The book is called I see you in my Dreams and there are only three physical, handmade copies in production. The only other location that you will be able to explore I see you in my Dreams is here, on this website.

The Concept

Since I was a little girl, I've always toyed with the fine line between my dreamworld and reality. It hasn't necessarily been on purpose- I have intense, vivid dreams that mimic my every day activities. I find myself confusing events from the past with dreams that assume the future. My memories aren't credible, I hold on to lies and mistruths and create my own relationships when asleep. For my book, I see you in my dreams, I wanted to artistically explore this realm by capturing real, every day moments with a dreamlike aura, a disposable camera. I call this mode of creative exploration sleepwalking through the city with my eyes wide shut. Although I'm alseep, I am still faced with every day situations in my dreams, blurring what I know to be real, creating misconceptions and false realities that may invalidate what's really real.
